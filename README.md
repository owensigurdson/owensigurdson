# Owen Sigurdson

**I build practical AI tools for the trades.**

Spent years running a contracting business in Alberta — fencing, decks, retaining walls, exterior work. Now I build the software I wish I'd had: tools that answer the phone, chase the leads, and price the jobs so small trade businesses don't bleed revenue while they're on the tools.

## 🎯 What I'm building

Right now I'm focused on becoming a developer. I'm working through Harvard's CS50x and building real projects alongside it — the goal is a Software Engineer role.

## 🧰 What I've built

- **[Pickup Technologies](https://use-pick-up.com)** — AI phone receptionist for home service businesses. Contractors miss roughly half their inbound calls because they're up a ladder or running a saw. Pickup answers, qualifies the lead, and books the job. Built with PIPEDA and Quebec Law 25 compliance baked in from day one.
- **Woden CRM** — Lead and job management built for a real contracting business (mine). Tracks leads from first call to final invoice.
- **Woden Estimator** — Estimating tool for fencing, decking, and exterior work. Material takeoffs, labour pricing, and client-ready PDF estimates.
- **Woden Hiring Manager** — Lightweight applicant tracking for trades hiring, where résumés are rare and reliability is everything.
- **[wodencontracting.com](https://wodencontracting.com)** — Lead-gen website for my contracting company, built end-to-end with Claude Code.

## 🪓 Why the trades angle

Most software for contractors is built by people who've never set a fence post in frozen ground. I ran the business, swung the hammer, did the estimates at 10pm, and missed the calls that cost me jobs. That's the gap I build for.

Also: former Canadian Armed Forces infantry section commander. I like systems that work under pressure.
